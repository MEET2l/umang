"Empowering Futures Through Early Childhood Excellence"

"Empowering Futures Through Early Childhood Excellence"

This motto encapsulates the essence of SpacECE's mission: to empower children and their families by providing quality early childhood education, fostering school readiness, and supporting the transition from early childhood to early grade learning. Through a focus on inclusive and equitable development, SpacECE aims to contribute to lifelong learning, breaking the cycle of inequity, and ensuring that all children have the opportunity to thrive.

SpacECE is dedicated to empowering children aged 0–8, the most critical years for growth and learning. We believe that strong foundations built through playful, engaging early childhood experiences shape lifelong success. Research shows that quality early learning reduces dropout rates, improves readiness, and enhances educational outcomes—an effort supported in India through ICDS and Anganwadi centres. Despite progress, gaps remain, which led to the creation of the National ECCE Policy (2013) and a major shift through the NEP 2020 that includes ECCE from age 3 and aims for universal quality early education by 2030. Aligned with NEP 2020 and the ECCE Policy, SpacECE works to strengthen systems, promote inclusive and equitable development, and support children’s readiness for school and lifelong learning.

Why Home-as-a-Learning-SPACE?

Home-as-a-Learning-SPACE is important for early years development because the early years of a child's life are critical for their growth and development. During these years, children are developing their language, cognitive, emotional, and social skills, and their environment plays a significant role in shaping these skills.

Creating a learning space at home can help to stimulate a child's curiosity, encourage exploration, and promote learning through play. This can be achieved by providing a safe, comfortable, and stimulating environment that is tailored to the child's interests, needs, and abilities.

When children are provided with a space that is conducive to learning, they are more likely to be engaged and motivated to explore, experiment, and learn. This, in turn, can help to build their confidence, resilience, and sense of autonomy, which are essential skills for success in school and in life.

Moreover, a home learning space can provide parents and caregivers with opportunities to engage with their children in meaningful and enjoyable learning activities. This can help to strengthen the bond between parents and children, promote positive parent-child interactions, and support children's social and emotional development.

Creating a home learning space is a simple yet effective way to support children's early years development and set them on a path towards lifelong learning and success.

Home-as-a-Learning-SPACE

SpacECE India Foundation has launched “Home-as-a-Learning-SPACE,” a unique program that promotes early childhood education through experiential, hands-on learning at home. Designed for children aged 1 to 3 years, the program equips parents and caregivers with activity guides, learning kits, and educational games to create an engaging and development-focused learning environment. By making learning fun, accessible, and interactive, the initiative supports the development of essential competencies in young children, especially those with limited access to quality early education. Through this effort, SpacECE aims to empower families, nurture young minds, and cultivate a lifelong love for learning across communities in India.
